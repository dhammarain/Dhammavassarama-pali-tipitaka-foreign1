One of the most important figures in the history of scholastic Chinese Buddhism. A monk who lived in Chang-an at the outset of the Tang dynasty. He became famous for his 17 year long trip to India, during which he studied with many famous Buddhist masters. When he returned, he brought with him some 657 Sanskrit texts.
